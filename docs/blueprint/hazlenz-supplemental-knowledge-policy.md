# HazLenz AI Supplemental Knowledge Policy

HazLenz AI may use supplemental safety knowledge to improve hazard reasoning, evidence-gap questions, corrective actions, and report quality. Supplemental references must be clearly separated from primary enforceable standards.

## Authority tiers

1. primary_enforceable
   - OSHA, MSHA, and directly applicable regulatory standards.
   - These may be shown as primary citations when scope and evidence support applicability.

2. incorporated_by_reference
   - Consensus or external standards incorporated into an enforceable regulation.
   - These may be elevated only when the applicable regulation, jurisdiction, or adopted rule supports that treatment.

3. supplemental_consensus
   - NFPA, ANSI, ASME, CGA, NEC/NFPA, and similar consensus standards.
   - These may support reasoning and controls but should not be presented as primary enforceable citations unless incorporated or adopted.

4. industry_guidance
   - NIOSH, ACGIH, manufacturer instructions, recognized industry practices, and safety guidance.
   - These may support exposure reasoning, evidence gaps, and control recommendations.

5. company_policy
   - Site-specific rules, client requirements, insurance requirements, and internal procedures.
   - These may be treated as applicable only when provided by the user or organization.

## Output rules

HazLenz should:
- Keep OSHA/MSHA or directly applicable regulations as primary standards when supported by evidence.
- Use supplemental knowledge to improve corrective actions, risk reasoning, and evidence-gap questions.
- Label supplemental references as "Supplemental guidance" or "Consensus guidance."
- Avoid saying a supplemental reference is legally required unless jurisdictional adoption, incorporation by reference, contract, or company policy is confirmed.
- Ask an evidence-gap question when supplemental authority may depend on adoption.

## Example wording

Primary standard:
- 29 CFR 1910.1200, Hazard Communication.

Supplemental guidance:
- NFPA, ANSI, DOT, CGA, or manufacturer guidance may support chemical labeling, storage, compatibility, fire prevention, transportation, cylinder handling, or emergency-control recommendations. Confirm whether the site, jurisdiction, contract, or company policy adopts these requirements.

## Target supplemental sources by hazard family

Hazard Communication / Chemicals:
- NFPA fire/flammable-liquid guidance
- DOT hazardous materials transportation references when shipment/transport is involved
- NIOSH/ACGIH exposure guidance
- Manufacturer SDS instructions

Compressed Gas:
- CGA guidance
- NFPA 55 where applicable
- DOT cylinder marking/transport context when transportation is involved

Electrical:
- NFPA 70 / NEC
- NFPA 70E for electrical safe work practices
- Manufacturer/listing instructions

Machine Guarding:
- ANSI B11 family and manufacturer guarding guidance
- Lockout/control-of-energy consensus practices where servicing is involved

Fall Protection / Walking-Working Surfaces:
- ANSI/ASSP fall protection and ladder guidance
- Manufacturer instructions for ladders, lifts, anchors, and fall-arrest systems

Fire / Emergency / Egress:
- NFPA life safety/fire prevention guidance
- Local fire code or AHJ requirements when known

Industrial Hygiene:
- NIOSH, ACGIH, AIHA, OSHA/MSHA sampling guidance
- Exposure limits and monitoring guidance should be advisory unless legally adopted.

Mining:
- MSHA remains primary.
- Manufacturer instructions, ANSI/ASSP, NIOSH, NFPA, and industry guidance may support controls depending on hazard type.
